First Aid for Mental Health: Schools and Universities

1-Day Training | Level 2 FAA Award

Course Overview

This specially designed, regulated one-day training course for schools and universities offers a nationally recognised Level 2 FAA Award and Qualification. The course is available through classroom-based or online delivery and is accompanied by a course manual.

The course will give participants the skills and tools to recognise the early signs of mental health conditions, an understanding of mental health, and the factors that can affect mental wellbeing.

The course will provide participants with a range of practical tools and techniques to help grow their confidence to support themselves, their colleagues, students, and peers. Participants will also learn how to prevent issues from arising by building a supportive culture, encouraging positive conversations, and creating a healthy climate around mental wellbeing.

Please note: Participants will not be taught how to diagnose or treat mental health conditions, as this can only be carried out by professionals who have the appropriate knowledge, experience, and qualifications.

Target Group

The regulated training course is suitable for all staff who work in education and anyone who actively engages with young adults working or studying in schools or universities.

Learning Outcomes

On completion of the training, the participant will be able to:

  • Know what mental health is
  • Why young people develop mental health conditions
  • The role of a First Aider for mental health
  • Know how to recognise and manage anxiety and stress
  • Understand the impact of Drugs and Alcohol on mental health
  • Understand a range of mental health disorders and the support/therapy provided by professional healthcare

Please note that this is the Youth First Aid for Mental Health course tailored specifically for school and university settings and retains the nationally recognised UK Level 2 FAA Award and qualification.
